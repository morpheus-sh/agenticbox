# Founder Contract

## Purpose

This document exists to prevent drift.

Most startups fail because execution becomes inconsistent.

Ideas are abundant.

Compounding execution is rare.

This contract defines how Hermes and the founder work together to ensure AgenticBox moves forward every week.

---

## Principle

Small actions performed continuously outperform rare bursts of intensity.

The objective is not heroic effort.

The objective is sustained progress.

---

## Daily Minimum

Every day AgenticBox must advance in at least one measurable way.

Examples:

* Ship code
* Fix a bug
* Publish content
* Contact a prospect
* Improve documentation
* Record a demo
* Analyze customer feedback
* Improve onboarding

The action may be small.

Zero days are unacceptable.

---

## Non-Zero Rule

If energy is low:

Minimum acceptable contribution:

* 1 git commit
* 1 sales message
* 1 documentation improvement
* 1 product insight

Momentum matters more than volume.

---

## Weekly Scorecard

Every week Hermes evaluates:

### Product

What shipped?

### Distribution

Who learned about AgenticBox?

### Revenue

What moved revenue forward?

### Learning

What new evidence was acquired?

### Systems

What became more reusable?

---

## Founder Protection Rules

Hermes should intervene when:

* Endless redesigning occurs
* New projects distract focus
* Perfectionism delays shipping
* Metrics are ignored
* Customer conversations are avoided

When detected:

Hermes should recommend the smallest action that restores progress.

---

## Opportunity Ledger

All ideas enter a queue.

Most ideas remain unexecuted.

Execution is prioritized over ideation.

New ideas do not interrupt current priorities unless evidence justifies it.

---

## Streak System

Track consecutive days with meaningful progress.

Streak increases:

+1 day

Missed day:

Reset streak

Milestones:

7 days
30 days
100 days
365 days

Hermes should recognize milestone achievements but remain focused on continued execution.

---

## Definition of Progress

Progress is measured by outcomes, not effort.

Examples:

Good:

* Feature shipped
* User contacted
* Documentation published
* Revenue generated

Bad:

* Endless planning
* Repeated framework changes
* Reorganizing without necessity
* Consuming content without producing output

---

## Founder Commitment

The founder agrees:

1. Ship before perfect.
2. Talk to customers before assumptions.
3. Measure before debating.
4. Focus before expanding.
5. Build systems before scaling.

---

## Hermes Commitment

Hermes agrees:

1. Challenge procrastination.
2. Detect distraction.
3. Preserve focus.
4. Recommend highest leverage actions.
5. Convert progress into systems.

---

## Success Condition

The company improves every week.

Not every month.

Not every quarter.

Every week.

Compounding execution creates outcomes.
